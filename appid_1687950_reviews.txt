Games like this are why I really wish that Steam would allow for an intermediate option between "Recommend" and "Not Recommend". Personally, I ironically find that the Roman number for 5, "V", perfectly represents my experience with this game. It starts with an absurdly high peak but during its  excessively long run time it gradually decreases in quality, dragging the whole experience down until it suddenly leaps in quality once again for its missable, final dungeon.

My opinion on the game is mixed. On the gameplay side, the combat is exactly as enjoyable as you’d expect from an Atlus/MegaTen title. Grinding is actually engaging this time around, thanks to side activities that meaningfully motivate exploration and progression rather than making it feel like a late-game chore. Compared to earlier entries, repetition is handled much better, and the main dungeons are a clear step up in level design, encouraging players to explore instead of rushing through. Visually, the game has a very strong and distinctive art direction that feels like a natural evolution of the series’ established style while still standing firmly on its own. And, unsurprisingly, the soundtrack is excellent.

Where the game stumbles for me is in the writing. While it’s far from terrible, I find it to be the weakest entry in the series in terms of narrative and character consistency. The story begins at an extremely high point with an opening arc that features one of the most effective and emotionally impactful antagonists the series has ever had. This early section feels grounded, unsettling, and painfully real, which makes it hit especially hard on an emotional level.

Unfortunately, the rest of the game struggles to live up to that standard. Later antagonists tend to feel more generic and less developed by comparison, lacking the same depth and presence. Because of this imbalance, the story gradually loses some of its emotional weight, and what follows can feel flatter or more cartoonish than intended. Some major story developments are also handled in a very predictable way, which reduces tension and makes certain plot threads feel underutilized.

The late game does recover somewhat with a stronger, more nuanced antagonist who stands out from the rest. While often described as morally complex, I personally found their motivations fairly straightforward. Still, what makes this character compelling is that they are genuinely well-intentioned rather than outright malicious, which adds a refreshing layer of tragedy and humanity to the conflict.
